Bespoke Design

Bespoke: "made specially, according to the needs of an individual customer." - Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary

This university project is about designing a gift experience for someone that is living far away from a friend or family member to help them maintain their long distance relationship. To be able to design this gift I went through the full design process: empathizing, defining, ideating, prototyping and finally testing. The results was a digital experience tailor-made to the persons personality and needs.

Research

Research Plan

To prepare for this project, I estabilished a reseach plan starting with constructing my reseacrh question to understand the gift receiver and their preferences, values, investigate as much as possible about personality traits, interests and needs and their relation with gift giving and/or receiving personal experiences.

Research Process

To answer the research questions and empathise with the user, I prepared a few research techniques that you can see below:

My Research Techniques

I have made use of 6 research techniques

User Interview

Semi-structured

Interviewed the user to understand his personality and personal needs.

Gift shop observation

2021-2022

Did an observation activity in a gift shop where I could observe his behaviour and gift preferences.

Cultural Probes

2021-2022

Sent the user a activity to do during the weekend where he had to describe his day and take pictures of interesting things he likes.

Personality test

Semi-structured

I applied a personality test Myers-Briggs Type Indicator where the user had to fill up a questionnaire with questions about his personality style.

Life Mapping technique

2021-2022

The user had to draw or describe important facts in his life showing a time-line of his life.

.

Sinterklaas surprise

2021-2022

I have made a gift for the user and had prepared the gift experience to see his reaction.
